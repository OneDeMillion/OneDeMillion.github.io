Tickets for Snowballs!

by Kim DeMille

One of the first images that springs to mind when we start seeing snowflakes falling from the sky is snowball fights. Many of us have very fond memories of carefully molding ball after ball into shape, and piling them high, ready to wage war in the age-old duals of the white flakes. However, did you know that there are city ordinances regarding snowball fights? If you don't follow city code you could find yourself with a ticket next time you run out and take battle with your friends at the park with your snowball maker in hand? Don't worry! We've got the scoop on what to do and not do to follow the rules and still have all the fun that snow days have to offer.

Tucked away in Preston municipal ordinances, right between two laws regarding the use of firearms in the city, there's a line about snowballs. Specifically, you can't throw them while on city property--including sidewalks, streets, parks and schools. "No person shall throw or shoot any object, arrow, stone, snowball or other missile or projectile, by hand or by any other means, at any other person or at, in or into any building, street, sidewalk, alley, highway, park, playground or other public place within the city,"

As Preston resident and fourth grader Jenny Gump put it, "that's a little strange".
